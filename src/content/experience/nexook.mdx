Nexook was an early-stage startup building a marketplace between experts and clients — psychologists, coaches, specialists — where the lead-to-appointment funnel was the core problem to solve. I joined as Senior Software Engineer in an advisor capacity while staying full-time at Smartbox. The engagement was deliberately broad: feature design, light management, frontend, backend, CI/CD, and infra — picking up wherever the team needed leverage.

Stack was Next.js + TypeScript + Tailwind on the frontend, Python + Django on the backend, AWS for hosting, Postgres for storage. The most interesting thing the CTO and I shipped together was a conversational AI that cloned the expert's voice to run pre-appointment discovery calls — qualifying and classifying leads before any of the expert's actual time was spent. Beyond that, I led a frontend architecture and design refactor and pushed code-quality bars across the team.

Nexook closed when the founders parted ways. It ended sooner than the engineering and the team deserved — I worked alongside the CTO and another senior engineer I learned a lot from, and the product had real signal before things came apart upstream.
